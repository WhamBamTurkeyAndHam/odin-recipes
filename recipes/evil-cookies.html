A WhamBam Special, The Worlds First: Milk and Tuna Mayo Cookies

Let's smash two cultral classics together. What could go wrong?

Ingredients:

Cookies:

  • 1 cup of butter, softened
  • 1 cup of white sugar
  • 1 cup of packed brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons of hot water
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of semisweet chocolate chips

Tuna Mayo Onigiri:

  • 1 cup of uncooked sushi rice
  • One can of Tuna
  • Japanese Mayonnaise
  • 1½ cups of water
  • Additional seperate water, about ¼ cup
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • 2 sheets nori (dry seaweed), cut into 1/2-inch strips
  • (Optional) 2 tablespoons of sesame seeds

Steps:

  1. Gather your ingredients, making sure your butter is softened, and your eggs are room temperature.
  2. Mix your Tuna with the Mayonnaise in a small bowl, set aside.
  3. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  4. Wash rice in a mesh strainer until water runs clear.
    The easiest method is to use a rice cooker, simply the rice and water into the rice cooker and turn on.
    Otherwise if you want to use a saucepan, combine washed rice and 1½ cups water in a saucepan.
    Bring to a boil over high heat, stirring occasionally. Reduce heat to low; cover, and simmer rice until water is absorbed, 15 to 20 minutes.
    Let rice rest for 15 minutes to continue to steam and become tender. Allow cooked rice to cool.
  5. Beat butter, white sugar, and brown sugar with an electric mixer in a large bowl until smooth.
  6. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  7. Dissolve baking soda in hot water. Add to batter along with salt.
  8. Stir in both the flour and chocolate chips.
  9. Drop spoonfuls of dough 2 inches apart onto ungreased baking sheets.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven until edges are nicely browned, about 10 minutes.
  11. Contemplate what got you here? Why are you in the kitchen following this recipe?
  12. Meanwhile, combine ¼ cup water with salt in a small bowl; use to dampen hands before handling rice.
    Divide cooked rice into equal portions. Should be about the size of your palm.
  13. Cool cookies on the baking sheets briefly before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
  14. Crush half of the cookies up, mixing it with your tuna mayonnaise mixture.
  15. Create a dimple in the rice and fill with a heaping teaspoon of the tuna mayonnaise/cookie micture.
    Cover with remaining portion of rice and press lightly to enclose filling inside rice ball.
    Gently press rice to shape into a triangle.
  16. Jam a cookie into the side of the Onigiri as garnish, wrap with a strip of nori and optionally (but not really) sprinkle with sesame seeds and cookie crumbs. Repeat with remaining portions of rice.
    Store in an airtight container or serve immediately and enjoy!
